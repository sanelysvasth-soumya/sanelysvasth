About BMR

What is Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR)?

BMR represents the minimum number of calories your body needs to perform basic life-sustaining functions while at rest. These functions include:

  • Breathing
  • Blood circulation
  • Cell production
  • Nutrient processing
  • Hormone regulation

Understanding Your Results:

Your BMR is the baseline number of calories you burn at rest. To determine your total daily calorie needs, multiply your BMR by your activity level factor:

  • Sedentary: BMR × 1.2
  • Light Activity: BMR × 1.375
  • Moderate Activity: BMR × 1.55
  • Very Active: BMR × 1.725
  • Extra Active: BMR × 1.9
