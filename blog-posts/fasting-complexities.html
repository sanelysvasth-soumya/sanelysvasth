Navigating the Complexities of Fasting: Fertility, Athletic Performance, and Health Implications

August 12, 2024 10 min read Health Science

Fasting has gained popularity as a lifestyle choice among health enthusiasts, athletes, and those seeking weight management. However, the relationship between fasting and various health aspects, including fertility, athletic performance, and overall health, is complex. In this blog, we'll delve into recent research regarding fasting's effects on fertility and athletic performance, and discuss its implications for pregnant individuals and those on medication. We will also address the potential pitfalls of fasting, ensuring you have a comprehensive understanding of the topic.

Fasting and Fertility: What the Research Says

One of the most pressing questions regarding fasting is its impact on fertility. Recent studies indicate that intermittent fasting may play a role in hormonal regulation. In premenopausal women with obesity, intermittent fasting has been found to decrease androgen markers, including testosterone, while increasing levels of sex hormone-binding globulin (SHBG). Notably, these effects were more pronounced when food intake was restricted to earlier in the day, such as before 4 PM.

Interestingly, fasting did not significantly alter estrogen, gonadotropins, or prolactin levels in women. For men, intermittent fasting may reduce testosterone levels in lean, physically active young males, yet SHBG levels remain unaffected. On a positive note, these reductions in testosterone didn't appear to impair muscle strength or mass. However, it's important to remember that the body of research on this topic is still limited, necessitating further studies to draw definitive conclusions.

Implications of Fasting on Athletic Performance

Fasting holds the potential to influence athletic performance, but it's a double-edged sword. On one hand, intermittent fasting can help athletes achieve and maintain an optimal body fat percentage while preserving lean muscle mass. A 2022 review highlighted benefits such as improved insulin sensitivity, reduced systemic inflammation, and bolstered immune function—key factors that can contribute to injury prevention and recovery.

However, the timing and type of fasting can make a significant difference. Some studies have shown detrimental effects on sprinting and cycling performance when athletes fast during certain periods, like Ramadan. That said, there's evidence suggesting that the body can adapt to fasting over a few days, allowing athletes to recover their performance levels.

For resistance training, there are concerns regarding potential muscle loss when fasting is coupled with high levels of physical activity. Athletes must remain mindful of how fasting approaches may affect their training and performance.

Fasting During Pregnancy and Breastfeeding: Tread Carefully

The implications of fasting become even more critical when considering pregnancy and breastfeeding. Current research suggests that long-term intermittent fasting before pregnancy could disrupt gut health in the offspring by decreasing beneficial microbiota and impairing intestinal barriers. Such disruptions can lead to metabolic issues in adult children.

Given these findings, it is paramount for pregnant or breastfeeding individuals to approach fasting with caution and consult healthcare professionals. The long-term impacts of fasting during these life stages remain largely unexplored, which adds to the need for awareness and careful consideration.

Fasting and Medications: Proceed with Caution

For individuals on medication, fasting could lead to unforeseen complications. Certain medications, like aspirin and iron supplements, can cause gastrointestinal distress when taken on an empty stomach. Antihypertensive medications may lead to dangerously low blood pressure during fasting, causing dizziness.

It's essential to consult with a healthcare provider to discuss whether adjustments to medication regimens are necessary before embarking on a fasting plan.

Potential Risks of Fasting

Though fasting can offer various benefits, it is not without its risks. One major concern is "post-restriction hyperphagia," where individuals may overeat after fasting periods, counteracting calorie deficits achieved during fasting.

Additionally, headaches and dizziness are common side effects for individuals new to fasting, often caused by dehydration or inadequate salt intake. To combat these symptoms, it's important to ensure proper hydration and, if needed, increase electrolyte intake.
